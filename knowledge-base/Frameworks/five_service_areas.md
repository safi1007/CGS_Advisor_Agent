# CGS Five Service Areas

## Overview

CGS Advisors organises its consulting services across five interconnected
areas that together span the full transformation journey. These five areas
are not independent products — they are interlocking capabilities that
address different dimensions of the same challenge: helping enterprise
leaders compete in a disrupted, connected world.

Strategic Transformation sits at the centre. The other four areas — Business
Strategy, Tech/Digital Strategy, Enterprise Innovation Enablement, and
Inertia Removal — each address a specific dimension of the transformation
challenge and connect back to the central strategic thread.

Most CGS engagements draw on two or more service areas simultaneously.
A client that needs a new corporate strategy (Business Strategy) almost
certainly also needs to address how technology will enable that strategy
(Tech/Digital Strategy) and how to overcome the organisational resistance
that will inevitably arise (Inertia Removal).

## 1. STRATEGIC TRANSFORMATION
*The centre of the CGS operating model*

### What It Is

Strategic Transformation is CGS's core offering and the hub through which
all other service areas connect. It is the end-to-end advisory capability
that helps organisations move from their current state to a transformed
state capable of competing in their disrupted industry.

Strategic Transformation engagements typically span 4–18 months and involve
working at all three levels of the CGS Transformation Framework — Industry
(Understand), Firm (Transform), and Executive (Lead). They address Clarity,
Capability, and Conviction simultaneously.

### What Problems It Solves

- The organisation knows it needs to transform but doesn't know where to
  start or how to prioritise
- Multiple transformation initiatives are underway but they're not
  coordinated or adding up to a coherent whole
- A previous transformation attempt failed or stalled and the organisation
  needs to reset
- The board or CEO needs an independent view of where the organisation
  stands relative to the disruption it faces
- The organisation is about to make a major strategic investment and wants
  to validate the strategic logic before committing

### Typical Deliverables

- Disruption Snapshot: CGS's assessment of where the industry is on the
  disruption continuum and where the client sits relative to peers
- Three Cs Assessment: Diagnostic of Clarity, Capability, and Conviction
  gaps and their root causes
- Transformation Strategy: A clear, grounded articulation of where the
  organisation needs to go and why
- Capability Roadmap: A prioritised, sequenced plan for closing capability
  gaps
- Transformation Programme Design: The architecture of the transformation
  initiative itself — governance, milestones, resourcing, and KPIs

### When CGS Recommends This Service Area

- When the client's challenge is organisation-wide rather than function-specific
- When the primary obstacle is strategic ambiguity or leadership alignment
  rather than execution
- When the organisation needs a comprehensive, integrated transformation
  approach rather than point solutions
- When the stakes are high enough to warrant senior CGS advisory involvement

## 2. BUSINESS STRATEGY & TRANSFORMATION
*Defining where to compete and how to win*

### What It Is

Business Strategy & Transformation focuses on the strategy formulation and
implementation work at the corporate, business unit, and functional levels.
It draws heavily on the Strategy Wheel — CGS's proprietary framework for
assessing and building an organisation's strategy capability — and addresses
the full cycle from external sensing and strategy formulation through to
execution and governance.

### What Problems It Solves

- The organisation lacks a clear, differentiated corporate strategy for the
  next 3–5 years
- A business unit is facing competitive pressure and needs a new strategy
  for its specific market
- The organisation's strategy exists on paper but is not being executed
  consistently or effectively
- There is no formal strategy function — strategy is developed ad hoc
  without consistent process or governance
- The organisation is considering a major strategic move (new market entry,
  acquisition, business model change) and needs rigorous strategic analysis

### Typical Deliverables

- Corporate or Business Unit Strategy: A complete strategy document
  addressing where to compete, how to win, and what capabilities are
  required, structured as an investment memo for executive and board
  decision-making
- Strategy Office Design: The structure, processes, and governance of a
  formal strategy function
- Digital Vision and Strategy: A specific strategy for how digital
  capabilities will support the broader business strategy
- Strategy Execution Framework: Translating strategy into operational
  priorities, KPIs, and accountability structures

### The CGS Strategy Wheel

CGS assesses business strategy capability across six dimensions:

**External Sensing**: The organisation's ability to monitor, interpret, and
respond to external market trends, competitive moves, and emerging
technologies. Low External Sensing is one of the primary root causes of
Clarity gaps.

**Internal Sensing**: The organisation's ability to honestly assess its own
capabilities, culture, performance, and readiness to execute its strategy.
Most organisations over-rate their internal capabilities — rigorous Internal
Sensing is a discipline that must be actively cultivated.

**Strategy Formulation**: The process by which the organisation develops
and updates its strategy. Best-in-class formulation processes are
collaborative, evidence-based, regularly updated, and produce clear
strategic choices rather than hedged, all-options-open statements.

**Strategy Communication**: How effectively the strategy is translated into
a message that employees at all levels can understand, connect to, and
act on. Most strategies are communicated far less effectively than their
authors believe.

**Strategy Execution**: The systems, processes, and disciplines that
translate strategic intent into operational action. Includes goal-setting
frameworks (OKRs, etc.), performance management, and resource allocation.

**Strategy Governance**: The structures and processes that keep the strategy
current, manage strategic decisions consistently, and ensure the
organisation's actions stay aligned with its strategic direction over time.

### When CGS Recommends This Service Area

- When the primary challenge is strategic clarity and direction rather
  than capability or execution
- When the client is a CEO, CSO, or business unit president facing a
  specific strategy challenge
- When the organisation needs to build or mature its strategy function

## 3. TECH / DIGITAL STRATEGY & TRANSFORMATION
*Aligning technology to business strategy*

### What It Is

Tech/Digital Strategy & Transformation addresses the technology dimension
of transformation — helping organisations develop IT and digital strategies
that are genuinely aligned to business strategy rather than driven by
technology trends, vendor relationships, or IT department priorities.

This service area recognises that technology transformation failure is almost
never a technology problem. It is a strategy problem (the technology isn't
connected to a clear business outcome), a people problem (the organisation
doesn't have the skills to deploy the technology effectively), or a process
problem (the technology is being deployed on top of processes that haven't
changed).

### What Problems It Solves

- The organisation has invested heavily in technology but hasn't seen the
  business results it expected
- IT and the business are misaligned — IT is building things the business
  doesn't want, and the business is going around IT to get things done
- The organisation needs an AI strategy but doesn't know how to develop
  one that goes beyond a list of AI tools
- The organisation's data architecture is fragmented and doesn't support
  the analytical capabilities the strategy requires
- The organisation is about to make a major technology investment (ERP
  replacement, cloud migration, AI platform) and needs to ensure it will
  actually deliver the intended business value

### Typical Deliverables

- IT Strategy: A technology strategy aligned to the business strategy,
  including investment priorities, architectural principles, and build/buy/
  partner decisions for key capabilities
- Digital Vision: A compelling, specific description of what the digitally
  transformed organisation will look and feel like — for customers,
  employees, and partners
- AI/Intelligence Strategy: A strategy for how AI and machine intelligence
  will be embedded in the organisation's operations, products, and decision-
  making — aligned to CGS's Intelligence Maturity Model
- Data Architecture Assessment and Roadmap: Diagnosis of current data
  fragmentation and a roadmap for building a unified data capability
- Digital Transformation Programme Design: The architecture of the
  technology transformation itself

### CGS's Distinctive Approach

CGS is not a technology consulting firm. It does not implement technology
solutions and it does not have preferred vendor relationships. This means
CGS's tech/digital strategy work is genuinely objective — focused on the
business outcomes the technology needs to enable rather than on selling
specific technology solutions.

CGS specifically calls out the difference between an AI Strategy (tactical
application of AI tools to existing processes) and an Intelligence Strategy
(fundamental redesign of how human and machine intelligence are configured
to create business value). CGS only helps clients develop Intelligence
Strategies — the former produces bolt-on innovation; the latter produces
transformation.

### When CGS Recommends This Service Area

- When the primary challenge is the alignment between technology and
  business strategy
- When the client is a CIO, CTO, or CDO facing a specific technology
  strategy challenge
- When the organisation needs to develop its AI or data capability
  strategically rather than tactically

## 4. ENTERPRISE INNOVATION ENABLEMENT
*Building the capacity to continuously innovate*

### What It Is

Enterprise Innovation Enablement helps organisations build the internal
capability to systematically generate, evaluate, develop, and scale
innovations — rather than relying on ad hoc ideas, external acquisitions,
or expensive innovation theatre (hackathons, innovation labs, and accelerators
that produce lots of activity but few results).

The core insight driving this service area is that innovation is not a
personality trait or a cultural aspiration — it is an organisational
capability that can be systematically built. Organisations that are
consistently innovative have better innovation processes, better governance,
better talent, and better connections to the external environment than
organisations that are not.

### What Problems It Solves

- The organisation talks about innovation constantly but produces few
  innovations that matter
- Innovation is concentrated in a small innovation team or lab rather
  than embedded across the organisation
- The organisation struggles to scale promising innovations from pilot to
  full deployment
- The organisation's innovation process is ad hoc, slow, and produces
  inconsistent results
- The organisation needs to develop new products, services, or business
  models to respond to industry disruption but doesn't have the capability
  to do so systematically
- The organisation is acquiring innovative companies but failing to
  integrate them effectively

### Typical Deliverables

- Innovation Capability Assessment: Diagnosis of the organisation's current
  innovation capability across process, people, culture, and governance
- Innovation Framework Design: A systematic process for generating,
  evaluating, and developing innovations, customised to the organisation's
  strategy and culture
- Innovation Portfolio: A structured inventory and evaluation of current
  innovation initiatives, with recommendations for which to accelerate,
  adjust, or stop
- Innovation Governance Design: The structures, roles, and processes that
  give innovation the management attention and resources it needs
- Connected Innovation Strategy: How to build innovation networks with
  customers, partners, startups, and academic institutions — leveraging
  the connected world to extend the organisation's innovation reach
- Innovation Culture Programme: Interventions to build the cultural norms
  — psychological safety, experimentation, learning from failure — that
  support sustained innovation

### The CGS Innovation Paradox

CGS has observed a consistent paradox in enterprise innovation: the
organisations that most need to innovate are usually the ones least capable
of doing so. Their success has created cultures that reward reliability and
punish failure, processes that optimise for efficiency rather than
exploration, and leaders whose careers were built on executing the existing
model rather than questioning it.

Breaking this paradox requires addressing innovation capability at the
system level — not just providing innovation training or standing up an
innovation lab, but redesigning the processes, incentives, and governance
structures that currently make innovation difficult.

### When CGS Recommends This Service Area

- When the organisation has a clear strategy but lacks the innovation
  capability to execute it
- When the client is a Chief Innovation Officer, Chief Strategy Officer,
  or business leader responsible for new product development
- When the organisation needs to build new business models or revenue
  streams in response to disruption

## 5. INERTIA REMOVAL
*Making transformation actually happen*

### What It Is

Inertia Removal is the service area that addresses the human and
organisational dimensions of transformation — the forces that prevent
even the best strategies from being executed. It draws on CGS's deep
expertise in organisational behaviour, change management, and leadership
development.

Inertia Removal is often the difference between a transformation that
succeeds and one that fails. Most organisations that fail to transform do
not fail because they had the wrong strategy or the wrong technology. They
fail because they could not overcome the Dominant Logic Inertia of their
leaders or the Structural Inertia of their organisation.

See the CGS Inertia Removal Playbook for detailed diagnostic and tactic
guidance on both inertia types.

### What Problems It Solves

- The strategy is clear and the capabilities exist, but the organisation
  is not actually changing
- Middle management is passively or actively resisting transformation
- Key leaders are publicly committed to the transformation but privately
  hedging
- Previous transformation attempts have failed due to organisational
  resistance and the organisation needs to understand why and how to
  do better this time
- The organisation's culture is fundamentally inhospitable to the speed,
  experimentation, and cross-functional collaboration that transformation
  requires
- A specific leader or leadership team needs coaching on how to lead
  through transformation

### Typical Deliverables

- Inertia Assessment: Diagnosis of the specific forms of inertia present —
  Dominant Logic vs Structural, their root causes, and their severity
- Inertia Removal Roadmap: A prioritised plan of specific interventions
  to address each identified form of inertia
- Stakeholder Alignment Programme: A structured approach to building
  genuine alignment (not just nominal consensus) among key stakeholders
- Change Management Architecture: The communications, engagement, and
  transition support structures that help the organisation move through
  transformation
- Executive Coaching: Direct coaching for individual leaders on the personal
  dimensions of leading transformation
- Culture Change Programme: Multi-year interventions to shift the
  organisation's cultural norms toward those that support sustained
  transformation

### The CGS Perspective on Change Management

CGS deliberately uses the term "Inertia Removal" rather than "Change
Management" because the latter has come to be associated with
communications plans and training programmes that sit alongside the
transformation but don't change the system. Inertia Removal is a harder
and more fundamental intervention — it addresses the incentives, structures,
governance, and mental models that create resistance, not just the
communications that accompany change.

CGS believes that most transformation initiatives spend too little time
and resource on Inertia Removal and too much on strategy and technology.
The bottleneck in most transformations is not the quality of the strategy —
it is the organisation's ability to execute it in the face of inertia.

### When CGS Recommends This Service Area

- When a transformation initiative is stalling despite a clear strategy
  and adequate resources
- When leadership alignment is fragile or declining
- When the organisation's culture is identified as a significant barrier
  to transformation
- When a specific leader or leadership team needs support in developing
  their transformation leadership capability

## How Aria Uses the Five Service Areas

**For Project Memory Q&A**: When a client asks about the work CGS did with
them, Aria references the specific service areas that were in scope for
their engagement and explains what work was done within each.

**For scoping new needs**: When a client describes a challenge they are
facing post-engagement, Aria maps it to the relevant service area(s) to
help them understand what kind of CGS support would address it.

**Escalation logic**: When a client's challenge clearly falls within one of
the five service areas and requires human judgment, Aria names the relevant
service area in its escalation recommendation. Example: "This sounds like
it requires fresh Inertia Removal work — specifically addressing the
Structural Inertia that's slowing your CDO integration. This is exactly
the kind of challenge where a CGS engagement would add the most value.
Want me to brief your CGS consultant on what you've described?"
