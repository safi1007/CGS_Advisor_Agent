## Purpose of This Document

This roadmap represents CGS Advisors' strategic vision for Meridian
Automotive Group's transformation journey through 2030. It goes beyond
the immediate Connected Vehicle Capability Programme (CVCP) — which
addresses the Ford 2027 deadline — and maps the full arc of what
Meridian must become to be a sustainable, growth-oriented Tier 1
supplier in the software-defined vehicle era.

This document is not an execution plan. It is a compass. It answers
the question: if Meridian executes the CVCP successfully and makes the
right strategic choices in the years that follow, what does Meridian
look like in 2030?

Aria uses this document to help Whitfield and the leadership team
keep near-term execution decisions connected to the long-term
strategic vision. Every milestone in the CVCP should be evaluated
against this roadmap: does completing this milestone on time move
Meridian closer to the 2030 vision or not?

## The 2030 Vision: From Tier 1 Supplier to Connected Vehicle Partner

### Where Meridian Is Today (February 2026)

Meridian is a world-class manufacturer of precision mechanical components.
Its competitive advantage is engineering quality, manufacturing reliability,
and 37 years of OEM relationships. It generates $2.41B in revenue, almost
entirely from one-time component sales. It has near-zero software capability.
Its entire business is at risk if it does not transform.

### Where Meridian Must Be by 2030

By 2030, Meridian must have completed a fundamental business model
transformation — from a component supplier to a connected vehicle
technology partner. Specifically:

**Revenue mix**: At least 25% of revenue ($750M+) from software-defined
and connected vehicle products and services, up from approximately 0%
today. This includes OTA-enabled component supply, vehicle data services,
and software licensing revenue.

**Capability position**: Recognised by Ford, BMW, Stellantis, and GM as
a Tier 1 SDV-capable supplier — not just compliant with minimum
requirements but genuinely differentiated in software integration capability.

**Intelligence maturity**: Organisational Intelligence Maturity at A4
(Collaborative) across all four dimensions — Strategy, People, Process,
and Technology. A4 means human and AI are genuinely complementary
collaborators, not AI as a bolt-on tool.

**Talent profile**: Software engineering headcount of 200+ FTEs (up from
75 at CVCP completion). An employer brand that competes effectively for
software talent against Aptiv, Bosch, and technology companies.

**Customer diversification**: Active SDV contracts with at least 3 of
the 4 primary OEM customers (Ford, BMW, Stellantis, GM), plus at least
one non-automotive connected device customer (industrial IoT, commercial
vehicles, or defence).

**Financial profile**: EBITDA margin maintained or improved despite
the transformation investment, driven by higher-margin software and
services revenue offsetting pricing pressure on traditional components.
Exit multiple (for Northlake or any future investor) at 8–10x EBITDA.

## The Four Strategic Horizons

CGS structures Meridian's 2026–2030 journey across four horizons.
Each horizon has a primary focus, key milestones, and a clear
definition of success.

---

### HORIZON 1: Survive and Prove (2026)
*"Meet the Ford deadline and demonstrate SDV capability is real"*

**Period**: May 2026 – April 2027
**Primary focus**: Execute the CVCP. Meet the Ford model year 2027
requirement. Prove to the organisation, the board, and the OEM customers
that Meridian can deliver software capability.

**This horizon is fully mapped in the CVCP roadmap document.**

**Definition of success at Horizon 1 end (April 2027)**:
- Ford VIP interface live and validated
- 75 software engineers operational
- ISO 21434 cybersecurity compliance certified
- OTA capability live across SmartChain, PowerSync, and SafeGuard
- Transformation Health Score: 65–75/100
- CDO in place and effective for 12+ months
- BlackBerry QNX partnership delivering technical value
- AWS data platform operational

**What must NOT happen in Horizon 1**:
- Missing the Ford April 2027 deadline — this is a binary outcome.
  Missing it by even 60 days risks the contract renewal.
- Losing the CDO candidate to a competitor before hire
- CEO Conviction reverting under execution pressure
- Northlake forcing an exit before the transformation is complete

**Horizon 1 risk level**: HIGH. This is the most dangerous period of
the transformation because Meridian has never done this before and the
consequences of failure are existential for the Ford relationship.

### HORIZON 2: Scale and Expand (2027)
*"Turn the Ford proof into a platform for growth across all OEMs"*

**Period**: May 2027 – December 2027
**Primary focus**: Leverage the Ford SDV capability to win equivalent
contracts with BMW and Stellantis. Scale the software engineering team
from 75 to 150 FTEs. Begin generating software licensing revenue.

**Key milestones for Horizon 2**:

**H2-1: BMW Equivalent Certification — June 2027**
BMW's equivalent of Ford's VIP platform (BMW's Open Mobility Cloud,
OMC) requires supplier certification from model year 2028 onwards.
Meridian's German operations (1,400 employees in Stuttgart and Munich)
are the primary delivery vehicle for BMW. The CDO must engage BMW's
OMC technical team no later than Q3 2026 to understand requirements
and begin the certification process.

Target: BMW OMC certification by June 2027.

**H2-2: Stellantis SDV Programme Entry — September 2027**
Stellantis has signalled SDV supplier requirements for model year 2028.
Based on current intelligence, Stellantis's requirements will be
similar to Ford's but with different data format specifications.
Meridian's existing Stellantis relationship (28% of revenue) gives it
a privileged position to engage early.

Target: Stellantis SDV supplier pre-qualification by September 2027.

**H2-3: Software Engineering Team — 150 FTEs — September 2027**
The Ford CVCP requires 75 FTEs. Serving BMW and Stellantis
simultaneously requires significantly more. CGS's projection:
150 FTEs by September 2027 to handle three concurrent OEM programmes.

**H2-4: First Software Licensing Revenue — December 2027**
Beyond OTA-enabled components, Meridian has an opportunity to generate
software licensing revenue from the IP it develops in the CVCP.
Specifically, the OTA update architecture and the telemetry interface
are potentially licensable to smaller Tier 2 suppliers who need SDV
capability but cannot build it themselves.

Target: First software licensing agreement signed by December 2027.
Revenue target: $5–15M in year 1 of licensing.

**H2-5: Transformation Health Score — 65/100 — December 2027**
Target dimension scores:
- Strategy: A3 (55) — Intelligence strategy embedded, CDO leading
- People: A3 (55) — 150 engineers, AI literacy programme running
- Process: A3 (55) — SDLC fully operational, CI/CD deployed
- Technology: A3 (55) — AWS platform mature, full OEM integrations
- Overall: 55/100 — Progressing to Advancing

**Definition of success at Horizon 2 end (December 2027)**:
- BMW OMC certification achieved
- Stellantis SDV pre-qualification achieved
- 150 software engineers operational
- First software licensing revenue generating
- Transformation Health Score 55–65/100
- Northlake either still invested (and pleased with trajectory) or
  exited at a strong multiple — either is acceptable at this point

### HORIZON 3: Lead and Differentiate (2028–2029)
*"Establish Meridian as a genuinely differentiated SDV technology partner"*

**Period**: January 2028 – December 2029
**Primary focus**: Move from SDV compliance (doing what OEMs require)
to SDV differentiation (doing things OEMs cannot easily find elsewhere).
Build recurring revenue streams. Reach Intelligence Maturity A4.

**Strategic shift in Horizon 3**: The SDV capability Meridian builds
in Horizons 1 and 2 will, by 2028, be table stakes for all major Tier 1
suppliers. Aptiv, Magna, and Bosch will all have equivalent OTA and VIP
integration capability. Meridian's competitive advantage cannot be based
on just having SDV capability — it must be based on having it better,
or having unique capabilities that others don't.

CGS has identified three potential differentiation vectors for Horizon 3:

**Differentiation Vector 1: Vehicle Data Intelligence**
Meridian's components generate significant real-time vehicle data. By
2028, Meridian could be processing telemetry from 500,000+ vehicles
across its OTA-enabled component base. This data has significant value:
for predictive maintenance, for warranty cost reduction, and for OEM
product development insights.

Meridian could build a vehicle data intelligence capability that provides
OEM customers with actionable insights from Meridian component performance
data — a service that goes beyond supplying the component to providing
ongoing intelligence about how that component is performing across the
fleet. This is a genuine differentiation that component-focused competitors
cannot easily replicate.

**H3-V1 Milestone**: Vehicle Data Intelligence product launched — Q2 2028.
Revenue target: $30–50M annually by 2029.

**Differentiation Vector 2: SDV Integration Services**
As the industry moves to software-defined vehicles, OEMs are struggling
with the complexity of integrating hundreds of SDV components from
different Tier 1 suppliers into a coherent vehicle software stack.
Meridian, as a supplier of multiple component families (SmartChain,
PowerSync, SafeGuard), has a unique cross-component integration
perspective.

Meridian could offer SDV Integration Services — a consulting and
engineering service that helps OEM customers integrate Meridian's
components with adjacent systems in the vehicle stack. This is a
services revenue model on top of the traditional product revenue.

**H3-V2 Milestone**: SDV Integration Services offering launched — Q4 2028.
Revenue target: $20–40M annually by 2029.

**Differentiation Vector 3: Non-Automotive Connected Device Expansion**
The software, OTA, and data infrastructure Meridian builds for
automotive is directly applicable to other connected device markets —
commercial vehicles (trucks, buses), agricultural equipment (connected
tractors, precision farming equipment), and industrial IoT (connected
manufacturing equipment).

These markets are earlier in their software disruption cycle than
automotive — creating an opportunity for Meridian to enter as an
experienced SDV supplier rather than as a learner.

**H3-V3 Milestone**: First non-automotive connected device contract
signed — Q2 2029. Revenue target: $50–100M by 2030.

**Key milestones for Horizon 3**:

**H3-1: Intelligence Maturity A4 Achieved — June 2028**
Target dimension scores:
- Strategy: A4 (80) — Intelligence strategy at board level, AI in
  strategic planning
- People: A4 (80) — 200+ engineers, all leaders AI-literate,
  human-AI collaboration embedded in all roles
- Process: A4 (80) — All core processes redesigned for human-AI
  collaboration, agentic AI in engineering workflows
- Technology: A4 (80) — Multi-cloud architecture, AI governance
  framework mature, real-time intelligence across operations
- Overall: 80/100 — Leading

**H3-2: Revenue Mix Milestone — December 2028**
Target: 15% of revenue ($380M) from software and services
(up from 0% in 2026 and approximately 3% at end of Horizon 2).

**H3-3: Software Engineering Team — 200 FTEs — June 2028**

**H3-4: First Vehicle Data Intelligence Customer — Q2 2028**

**H3-5: Non-Automotive First Contract — Q2 2029**

**Definition of success at Horizon 3 end (December 2029)**:
- Transformation Health Score: 75–85/100 (Leading)
- 200+ software engineers
- Revenue from software and services: 15–20% of total
- Recognised by industry analysts as a differentiated SDV supplier
- At least one non-automotive connected device revenue stream active
- Intelligence Maturity at A4 across all dimensions

### HORIZON 4: Sustain and Compound (2030 and beyond)
*"CGS Momentum's role ends — Meridian is self-sustaining"*

**Period**: 2030 onward
**Primary focus**: By 2030, Meridian should have built the internal
capability — the strategy, people, processes, and technology — to
navigate future disruptions without external advisory support for
basic transformation questions. CGS Momentum's role evolves from
active monitoring to periodic strategic review.

**The 2030 ambition**:
- Revenue: $3B+ with 25%+ from software and services ($750M+)
- Transformation Health Score: 80–90/100 (Leading to Pioneering)
- Software engineering: 250+ FTEs
- Intelligence Maturity: A4 across all dimensions, elements of A5
  emerging
- Industry position: Top 5 globally recognised SDV-capable Tier 1
  supplier
- Business model: Genuine hybrid — traditional component manufacturing
  plus recurring software and services revenue

**What Meridian needs to have built internally by 2030 to be
self-sustaining**:
- A Strategy function with genuine external sensing capability —
  so Meridian is never again blindsided by a technology shift the
  way it was blindsided by the Ford SDV notification in 2024
- A CDO and senior leadership team capable of leading the next
  transformation horizon without external advisory support for the
  basics
- An Innovation capability that systematically scans for and responds
  to emerging disruptions in the automotive and connected device markets
- An Inertia Removal capability within the organisation itself —
  leaders and HR systems that can identify and address organisational
  resistance without CGS being in the room

## The Critical Path to 2030

CGS has identified the five decisions that will most determine whether
Meridian reaches the 2030 vision. These are not milestones — they are
judgment calls that no roadmap can make for Meridian's leadership.
Aria should help Whitfield and the executive team think through these
decisions as they approach.

**Decision 1: The CDO's mandate in Year 2 (2027)**
After the Ford deadline is met in April 2027, the CDO will face a choice:
consolidate and optimise the SDV capability built during the CVCP, or
immediately pivot to scaling for BMW and Stellantis. The right answer
is aggressive scaling — but it requires sustained CEO and board support
for continued investment at a time when the existential urgency of the
Ford deadline has passed. Conviction must be actively maintained even
after the burning platform is resolved.

**Decision 2: Build vs acquire for Horizon 2 scaling**
Going from 75 to 150 software engineers organically will take 18–24
months. Acquiring a small automotive software company (there are several
in Michigan and Germany at the $15–40M valuation range) could accelerate
this to 6–9 months. The make vs buy decision for talent at the Horizon 1
to 2 transition will materially affect the Horizon 2 timeline.

**Decision 3: Which differentiation vector to pursue in Horizon 3**
Vehicle Data Intelligence, SDV Integration Services, and non-automotive
expansion are not all equally achievable simultaneously. Meridian will
need to choose 1–2 to pursue seriously and sequence them. This is
a strategic choice that depends on where Meridian's emerging capabilities
are strongest and where OEM demand is clearest. CGS's current
recommendation: Vehicle Data Intelligence first (leverages existing
data infrastructure investment), non-automotive second (diversifies
revenue base), SDV Integration Services third (requires the most
additional capability to build).

**Decision 4: The Northlake exit and next ownership structure**
Northlake Growth Capital will exit at some point between 2027 and 2029.
How that exit is structured — a sale to strategic buyer, a new PE
investor, an IPO, or a Hargrove family buyout — will significantly
affect Meridian's capital availability and strategic flexibility in
Horizon 3. CGS recommends that Webb and Hargrove begin planning the
ownership transition in 2027, not when Northlake forces the conversation.

**Decision 5: When to shift Meridian's identity**
At some point between 2027 and 2029, Meridian will need to make an
explicit choice about how it describes itself — to customers, to
potential employees, and to investors. "Tier 1 automotive supplier
with SDV capability" is not the same brand as "connected vehicle
technology partner." The identity shift is not just marketing — it
is a signal to the organisation, to OEM customers, and to the talent
market about what Meridian is becoming. Making this shift too early
risks alienating the traditional manufacturing organisation. Making it
too late means missing the talent and customer opportunity that comes
with being an early self-identified software company in a hardware world.

## How Aria Uses This Roadmap

**Connecting present to future**: When Whitfield or the executive team
makes a near-term decision, Aria should connect it to this long-term
roadmap. Example: "Hiring a CDO with automotive-only experience will
meet Horizon 1 requirements but may limit Meridian's ability to pursue
the non-automotive expansion in Horizon 3. Is it worth expanding the
search criteria?"

**Flagging drift**: If Meridian's execution decisions appear to be
drifting away from the 2030 vision — for example, if budget cuts are
consistently reducing software engineering investment below the levels
needed to reach 200 FTEs by 2028 — Aria should name the drift
explicitly: "The current hiring pace projects to 140 FTEs by Q3 2028,
which is below the 200 FTE threshold CGS identified as required for
Horizon 3 differentiation. Is this a deliberate reprioritisation or
a resource constraint that can be addressed?"

**Providing context for difficult decisions**: When Whitfield faces a
decision that has long-term implications, Aria should surface the
relevant Horizon context. Example: If Whitfield asks about whether to
accept a short-term cost reduction that would delay the data platform
by 3 months, Aria can frame it: "A 3-month delay to the AWS platform
pushes the Vehicle Data Intelligence launch from Q2 2028 to Q3 2028 —
which is still within the Horizon 3 window, but compresses your time
to establish a revenue base before the 2029 non-automotive expansion."

**Not replacing human strategic judgment**: This roadmap is a tool,
not a script. Meridian's environment will change between now and 2030
in ways that CGS cannot predict. OEM requirements will evolve. New
competitors will emerge. New technologies will create new opportunities.
Aria's job is to help Whitfield and the leadership team maintain
strategic coherence as the environment changes — not to mechanically
enforce a plan written in February 2026.

When a decision requires genuinely fresh strategic thinking — evaluating
an acquisition opportunity, responding to a major competitive move,
reassessing the differentiation vector in Horizon 3 — Aria should
escalate to a human CGS consultant rather than attempting to resolve
it from the knowledge base. The roadmap gives Aria the context to
identify when a decision is routine execution (Aria can handle) vs
when it is a genuine strategic inflection point (human consultant
required).
