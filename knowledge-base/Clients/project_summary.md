## Why CGS Was Engaged

### The Triggering Event

In March 2024, Ford Motor Company notified Meridian that beginning in
model year 2027, all Tier 1 suppliers bidding on powertrain and chassis
contracts would be required to demonstrate software-defined vehicle (SDV)
integration capability as a prerequisite to bidding. Specifically, Ford
required:

1. Demonstrated ability to deliver Over-The-Air (OTA) update capability
   for supplied components
2. A data telemetry interface compatible with Ford's Vehicle Intelligence
   Platform (VIP)
3. A dedicated software engineering team of minimum 50 FTEs with
   AUTOSAR Adaptive and C++ expertise

At the time of notification, Meridian had 0 of these three requirements
in place. Their two software development offices (opened in 2024) employed
a total of 14 software engineers, none with automotive-specific software
expertise. Their revenue from Ford represented 34% of total revenue —
approximately $820M annually.

The CFO, Marcus Webb, immediately recognised this as a potential existential
risk. He brought it to CEO Daniel Hargrove and the executive team in April
2024. The initial reaction from CEO Hargrove was scepticism: "Ford has been
changing supplier requirements for 40 years. Half of these mandates never
get enforced." COO Linda Petersen agreed with the CEO. VP Digital Strategy
James Whitfield (the primary CGS contact) and CFO Marcus Webb were
significantly more alarmed.

This disagreement at the executive level was the first clear signal of a
Dominant Logic Inertia problem — and the primary reason CGS was engaged.

### How CGS Was Engaged

James Whitfield had encountered CGS through a keynote presentation by
Gregg Garrett at the Automotive Industry Action Group (AIAG) conference
in Detroit in May 2024. The presentation, "The Software-Defined Vehicle
and the Tier 1 Supplier's Existential Choice," directly addressed
Meridian's situation.

Whitfield contacted CGS in June 2024. Initial scoping conversations
revealed the Three Cs diagnostic picture that would be confirmed in the
full engagement:
- **Clarity Gap**: HIGH — fundamental disagreement at CEO/CFO/COO level
  on urgency
- **Capability Gap**: CRITICAL — near-zero software-defined vehicle
  capability
- **Conviction Gap**: MEDIUM — CFO had conviction, CEO/COO did not yet

CGS was formally engaged in September 2024. The engagement was sold as a
Strategic Transformation programme spanning 18 months at a total fee of
$1.85M. Lead consultant: Sarah Chen, Senior Managing Director.

## Three Cs Diagnostic Findings

### CLARITY GAP — Severity: HIGH (resolved to LOW by Phase 2)

**Finding**: At the start of the engagement, the Meridian executive team
was deeply divided on two fundamental questions:

*Question 1: How quickly must Meridian develop SDV capability?*
- CEO Daniel Hargrove: "5–7 years. The OEMs will extend timelines as
  they always do. We have time to build this gradually."
- CFO Marcus Webb: "2–3 years maximum. Ford's VIP platform launches in
  model year 2027. That's our hard deadline."
- COO Linda Petersen: Aligned with CEO.
- VP Digital James Whitfield: Aligned with CFO.

The CGS external sensing analysis confirmed Webb and Whitfield's position.
Ford's VIP platform was already in production validation. BMW had an
equivalent requirement rolling out across European suppliers from 2026.
Stellantis had signalled similar requirements in private briefings.

*Question 2: Is this a technology problem or a business model problem?*
- Technology view (CEO, COO, VP Engineering): "We need to hire some
  software engineers and add OTA capability to our existing products."
- Business model view (CFO, Whitfield): "This is a fundamental shift in
  what we sell — from one-time components to ongoing software-defined
  capability."

CGS's analysis confirmed the business model view.

**Root Cause**: Classic Dominant Logic Inertia in the CEO and COO. Both
had built successful careers in the traditional Tier 1 model. Their
mental model was not wrong about the old world — it was preventing them
from accurately reading the new one.

**Resolution**: CGS ran a two-day Burning Platform workshop in October
2024 with the full executive team. The workshop included:
- Live briefing from Ford and BMW procurement leads on actual SDV timelines
- A CEO-to-CEO conversation between Hargrove and Visteon's CEO Paul
  Garland, facilitated by Gregg Garrett
- Financial modelling by David Park showing revenue at risk under three
  scenarios

The financial modelling was decisive. Under the no-response scenario,
Meridian's Ford revenue drops from $820M to approximately $180M by 2029.
Including the cascade to BMW and Stellantis, total revenue at risk by
2029: $1.1B annually.

Hargrove left the workshop stating: "The question isn't whether to act —
it's how fast and how boldly."

**Post-workshop Clarity status**: Executive team aligned on:
- Timeline: Act with urgency for a 2026 capability delivery
- Scope: Business model transformation, not just a technology upgrade
- Priority: Connected Vehicle Capability is the #1 strategic priority

### CAPABILITY GAP — Severity: CRITICAL

CGS assessed Capability across the four Intelligence Maturity dimensions:

**Strategy Dimension: A2 (Augmentation)**
Meridian had a functioning strategy process for its traditional business.
Its digital and intelligence strategy was entirely absent. No articulation
of how technology would create competitive advantage in the SDV world.
No AI governance framework. No intelligence strategy.

**People Dimension: A1 (Replacement)**
The most critical capability gap and the most significant risk to the
transformation timeline:
- Software engineering headcount: 14 FTEs total, none with AUTOSAR
  Adaptive expertise. Ford requires 50+ FTEs.
- No Chief Digital Officer or equivalent.
- Average time to hire software engineers: 127 days (benchmark: 60–80).
- CEO and COO AI literacy: A1. CFO, Whitfield: A2.
- Culture: Strong hardware engineering culture. Near-zero software
  culture. Not compatible without deliberate intervention.

CPO Sandra Okafor in CGS interview: "We have no idea how to compete
for software talent. Our employer brand, compensation structures, career
paths — none of it is designed for the people we now desperately need."

**Process Dimension: A2 (Augmentation)**
- No software development lifecycle (SDLC) process.
- No OTA update process — end-to-end capability did not exist.
- No vehicle data collection or data architecture capability.
- Quality assurance processes designed for physical components.
  Entirely inadequate for software.
- Programme management: Strong for hardware (3–5 year cycles).
  Weak for software (2-week sprint cycles).

**Technology Dimension: A2 (Augmentation)**
- Data infrastructure: Fragmented. 14 separate data systems with no
  unified data platform.
- No cloud-native development environment, CI/CD pipeline, or automated
  testing infrastructure.
- No automotive cybersecurity capability (ISO 21434 compliance —
  required for SDV suppliers — not achieved).
- No relationships with automotive software platforms (AUTOSAR,
  BlackBerry QNX, AWS Automotive).

**Overall Capability Assessment at Engagement Start**:
- Strategy: A2 (30 points)
- People: A1 (10 points)
- Process: A2 (30 points)
- Technology: A2 (30 points)
- **Overall Health Score: 25/100 — Early Stage**

### CONVICTION GAP — Severity: MEDIUM (resolved to LOW by Phase 2)

At engagement start, Conviction was fragile and unevenly distributed.
CFO and VP Digital were genuinely convinced. CEO and COO were not.

The Burning Platform workshop in October 2024 resolved the CEO's
Conviction gap. The Northlake board alignment session in November 2024

Residual Conviction risk: not executive but board-level. The Hargrove

**Post-alignment Conviction status**: Assessed as LOW risk by end of
Phase 2, November 2024.

## Key Recommendations Delivered

### Recommendation 1: Hire a Chief Digital Officer by May 31, 2026

**Rationale**: The transformation requires a senior leader with deep
software industry experience to lead the SDV capability programme,
attract software talent, and bridge the gap between Meridian's hardware
DNA and the software capability it needs to build.

**Specific requirements**:
- 15+ years in software/technology, at least 5 in automotive software
- Experience building software engineering teams of 50–200 FTEs
- Deep knowledge of AUTOSAR, ISO 21434, ASPICE
- Track record in high-velocity software environment
- Compensation: $380,000–$480,000 base + equity programme

**Status at engagement close (February 2026)**: CDO search in progress.
Three candidates shortlisted. Spencer Stuart engaged as search firm.
Expected hire: May 2026.

*This is the single most important milestone in the entire roadmap.*

### Recommendation 2: Launch Connected Vehicle Capability Programme (CVCP)

**Programme scope**:
- Build SDV Software Engineering team of 75 FTEs by December 2026
- Achieve AUTOSAR Adaptive certification for 20 engineers by
  September 2026
- Develop OTA update capability for three product families
  (SmartChain, PowerSync, SafeGuard) by October 2026
- Achieve ISO 21434 cybersecurity compliance by October 2026
- Develop Ford VIP-compatible telemetry interface by December 2026

**Programme investment**: $12.4M over 14 months (May 2026–April 2027)
- Talent acquisition and compensation: $6.8M
- Technology infrastructure: $2.9M
- Training and certification: $0.8M
- External partnerships and vendor relationships: $1.9M

**Status at engagement close**: Programme design complete. Governance
structure agreed. Bridget Larson engaged as interim programme director.
First hires in process (6 engineers hired as of February 2026).
Formal programme launch: May 2026.

### Recommendation 3: Data Infrastructure Phase 1 — Invest $3.2M

**Phase 1 scope**:
- Unified data platform on AWS (selected for Ford VIP compatibility)
- Real-time vehicle telemetry ingestion capability
- Data governance framework
- Basic vehicle performance analytics dashboard

**Status at engagement close**: Board approved. AWS selected. Slalom
engaged as implementation partner. Contract signature target: May 2026.
Expected platform live: September 2026.

### Recommendation 4: BlackBerry QNX Strategic Partnership

**Rationale**: Meridian cannot build all SDV software capability from
scratch within the available timeframe. QNX partnership accelerates
capability development by 12–18 months.

**Investment**: $1.9M per year
**Status at engagement close**: Letter of Intent signed January 28, 2026.
Full agreement expected April 2026.

### Recommendation 5: Culture and Talent Brand Programme

**Recommended actions**:
- Employer brand refresh targeting software engineers
- Compensation adjustment: software engineering pay currently 18% below
  market — must reach parity minimum
- New software engineering career ladder
- Remote/hybrid policy: maximum 2 days per week in-office for software
  engineers (policy approved by CEO February 2026 — significant win)

**Status at engagement close**: CPO Sandra Okafor owns this workstream.
Employer brand agency briefed. Compensation analysis commissioned. Remote
work policy approved and effective immediately.

### Recommendation 6: Executive Alignment Workshop
**Delivered**: October 2024. See Clarity Gap section.

### Recommendation 7: Board and Investor Alignment
**Delivered**: November 2024. See Conviction Gap section.
